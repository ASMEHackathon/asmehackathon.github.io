About

     The SDSU Hackathon is part of a Big Data initiative at SDSU led by Ming-Hsiang (Ming) Tsou, Professor in Geography and Director of Center for Human Dynamics in the Mobile Age.

     SDSU wants to host a “Big Data For San Diego” Hackathon at SDSU that brings together educators, students, programmers, journalists, public officials, local government and the wider San Diego community to examine an important civic issue with actionable results. With this, we hope to build a collaborative community for the future development of Open Government and Open Data Initiative for San Diego.


Hackathon Coordinators and Contacts:

Amy Schmitz Weiss (aschmitz@mail.sdsu.edu, Associate Professor in the School of Journalism & Media Studies, SDSU)
Ming-Hsiang (Ming) Tsou (mtsou@mail.sdsu.edu, Professor in Geography and Director of Center for Human Dynamics in the Mobile Age)


Goals and Expected Outcomes:

1. To identify important civic issues in the San Diego region that can be potentially explored or solved through the lens of Big Data Science and Software Development.
2. To build a community of educators, students and the wider San Diego community around a specific civic issue that has far-reaching impacts for the future development of Open Government and Open Data in San Diego.
3. To contribute to the initiative of the Big Data Science Education Program (certificate programs and a possible MS program at SDSU).
4. To encourage Hispanic and minority students to learn computer science and programming skills for their future careers (SDSU is a Hispanic-serving Institute).
